The main forming method of composite structural parts in the aerospace field-autoclave forming method

Category: Company headlines    release time: 2019-10-24    Author：admin
  share to:   
QR code sharing


In recent years, the autoclave molding method has been regarded as a "high-cost" composite material structure manufacturing method, and has been excluded from the "low-cost method" concept system. The resin transfer method has formed a new and old contrast with it, which seems to be hot pressing The can forming method should be eliminated. In fact, the molding method of autoclave is still the main molding method of composite structural parts in the aerospace field in terms of maturity and scale in engineering application, which is determined by its characteristics:


(1) The pressure and temperature in the tank are uniform. Under their joint action, it can meet the requirements of high fiber content in composite materials. The composite materials have high mechanical properties and relatively stable physical properties. For example, the porosity of the composite material structure is low and the resin content is uniform.

（2）热压罐成型方法适用范围广。例如层压结构、夹芯结构、胶接结构和缝纫结构。模具相对比较简单，效率高，尤其适用于大型的具有高性能要求的复合材料结构件的成型。

In order to meet the development of aircraft resin-based composite materials, according to incomplete statistics, domestic aviation main engine manufacturers have more than 30 autoclaves, of which 1/3 are imported from abroad, with a production area of ​​200,000 square meters, and most of the equipment The technical parameters and plant conditions can meet the requirements for the production of civil aircraft composite material structural parts. Based on this molding method, a large number of experimental studies have been conducted in material modification, process parameter optimization, thickness size and deformation control, mold design and manufacturing, non-destructive testing, damage repair, etc. The results have been verified in engineering. And formed relevant standards and specifications. In recent years, aircrafts have more stringent requirements on the performance of composite materials. In order to improve the production efficiency of composite material structural parts and the consistency of the quality of composite material structural parts, each host manufacturer has successively purchased special composite material design/manufacturing software (such as Fibersim Software) and the matching prepreg automatic blanking machine, laser projector, automatic C-scan non-destructive testing equipment and five-axis CNC drilling and milling equipment. Some aviation companies have also purchased or are purchasing automatic tape laying machines, ultrasonic honeycomb milling machines, and numerical control processing equipment with flexible fixtures. A complete digital production line for composite material structure design/manufacturing is being opened up, and the production capacity of composite material structures with an annual output of more than 85t has been formed. Therefore, at least 1/2 of the remaining productivity can be used for the production of large aircraft composite structures.

As for how to reduce the cost of the autoclave molding method, a lot of research work has been done in China, such as the realization of "zero glue absorption" of prepregs, the localization of auxiliary materials, the improvement of the thermal efficiency of the co-curing mold structure and the improvement of the assembly form. Optimization, the reasonable combination of structural parts into the tank, and the rationalization of furnace parts test items have effectively promoted the reduction of manufacturing costs. Take a certain aircraft composite material structure as an example, every 1kg of composite material structure produced can save more than 1,200 yuan in manufacturing cost. If prepreg layup is changed to automatic layup in the manufacture of large composite material structures, production efficiency will be significantly improved and manufacturing costs will continue to decrease. Therefore, it is inaccurate to regard it as a "high cost" manufacturing method. After more than 30 years of technological development, this molding method has undoubtedly been important for the application of composite materials in large aircraft structures to enter the fast lane. significance.

In addition, due to the advantages of the vacuum assisted molding (VARI) process in reducing the cost of composite materials and the overall molding of large components, it has received extensive attention in the industry in recent years. Relevant domestic units have developed a resin system suitable for the VARI process, verified its process method, studied the performance of its composite materials, and trial-produced related typical parts. Although the performance of the composite material produced by this process is basically equivalent to that of the autoclave method, there is still a lot of research work to be done to meet the requirements of the high-performance composite material structure. Therefore, at this stage, the advantages of autoclave pressure and solidification should be used to make up for the lack of vacuum pressurization in the VARI process, and to maximize strengths and avoid weaknesses. This is also an effective technical way to reduce the manufacturing cost of autoclave molding methods.
