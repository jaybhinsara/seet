LANXESS' continuous fiber-reinforced thermoplastic composite material won the gold medal of the European automotive lightweight project

Category: Company headlines    release time: 2019-12-24    Author：admin
  share to:   
QR code sharing

LANXESS’s continuous fiber-reinforced thermoplastic composite material Tepex Dynalite has made an important contribution to the success of the FuPro project of the German Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF). At the European New Energy Vehicle Expo held in Munich, Germany, the project won the Gold Award in the Materialica "Surface Technology" category. FuPro stands for "Design and process development of functional multi-component structure combined with composite hollow profiles".

Tepex dynalite Composite materials for seat backs

       The innovative fiber composite material modular system of the research project combines organic sheets and injection molded composite materials to form a highly integrated multi-component structure. The example of the integrated seat belt backrest proves that this technology has high application potential. The organic sheet used for this is made of a semi-finished product of Tepex dynalite 102-RG600, which is based on glass fiber roving and a polyamide 6 matrix. Bond-Laminates, a subsidiary of LANXESS in Brilon, Germany, produces these extremely lightweight and extremely elastic fiber composite semi-finished products.


       Innovative products made of Tepex materials have won the Materialica Award in 2014 and 2017. The components used in the two award-winning products are now in mass production.

FuPro project

       In the FuPro project, an interdisciplinary team from industry and science has developed a new technology that applies continuous fiber composite hollow profiles to mixed organometallic plate injection molding structures. The units participating in the project include the Institute of Lightweight Engineering and Polymer Technology (ILK) of the Dresden University of Technology and Brose Automotive Parts Co., Ltd., as well as Arburg, AUMO, DITF Denkendorf, Elring Klinger, GK Concept, and Jiwei K, Schmalz, Boreme fiber and Werkzeugbau Siegfried Hofmann.

       The FuPro research project aims to develop and analyze a new mass production process applied to a multi-component structure made of composite fiber reinforced plastic (FRP) hollow profiles, organic plates and injection molding compounds. Its purpose is to make the level of integration of technology, structure and function much higher than traditional design methods, thereby significantly reducing the weight of the vehicle structure.

Lightweight design as a key technology

As a key technology, lightweight design is a necessary prerequisite for resource-efficient motorization. The highly integrated multi-component engineering method, that is, the combination of torsion-resistant hollow sections, planar structural elements and complex node structures, is extremely conducive to the realization of high-load lightweight structures. In addition, the use of fiber-reinforced plastic can optimize individual components based on load distribution. Thermoplastic fiber reinforced plastics are bound to be mass-produced in the automotive industry because their parts usually have a production cycle of less than one minute.


About LANXESS

LANXESS is a global supplier of specialty chemicals with total sales of 7.2 billion euros in 2018. It has approximately 15,500 employees worldwide and is distributed in 60 production bases in 33 countries. The core business of LANXESS includes the development, production and sales of chemical intermediate products, additives, specialty chemicals and plastics. LANXESS has been included in the Dow Jones Sustainability Index (DJSI Global and Europe) and the FTSE4Good Index (FTSE4Good).
