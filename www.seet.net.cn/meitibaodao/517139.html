Thermoplastic composite pipes successfully applied to the Gulf of Mexico oil field

Category: Company headlines    release time: 2019-12-27    Author：admin
  share to:   
QR code sharing

On December 17, British company Magma Global announced that it had been selected by American C-Innovation to provide m-pipe thermoplastics for the latter’s offshore oilfield projects in the Gulf of Mexico.Composite materials submarine bridging pipes. The m-pipe has a diameter of 4 inches and a pipe pressure of 68.95MPa. It is expected to start erection in the next few months.



C-Innovation plans to use Flexi-Coil to clean up and remediate suspicious blockages and obstacles in the subsea production system. This work involves many different locations in the Gulf of Mexico. The depth can reach 6,500 feet (about 5,029 meters). During the operation, the drill pipe riser will be lowered through the moonpool of the offshore support vessel, and then the m-pipe will be bridged to connect to the subsea infrastructure in a free-hanging high-curvature waveform pattern. The Flexi-Coil will descend along the drill pipe riser and enter the oil outlet pipe through the m-pipe jumper.


m-pipe is a thermoplastic composite pipe developed by Magma Global. It is a continuous and flexible connecting pipe made of carbon fiber reinforced polyether ether ketone (PEEK) composite material by laser welding. The jumper produced by this structure is lightweight, high-strength, and can withstand extremely high bending strains. In addition, the material is extremely resistant to corrosion in the subsea environment and has strong resistance to various oil field chemicals. The flexible m-pipe jumper can eliminate the ups and downs caused by the turbulence of the drilling ship and provide a smooth path for the entry of the Flexi-Coil equipment.
