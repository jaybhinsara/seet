The composite "black" wing of the MC-21 aircraft

Category: Company headlines    release time: 2020-06-16    Author：admin
  share to:   
QR code sharing
MC-21 is a new type of passenger aircraft designed by Russia in recent years. The Russian aviation industry hopes to use this aircraft to compete with Boeing-737 and Airbus A-320 passenger aircraft for the civil aviation market.。
 
The major innovation of MC-21. lies in its composite wing called the "black" wing. This wing is critical to achieving the aircraft's "high efficiency, low resistance, and high lift" performance. In order to create a brand-new "black" wing, Russia established the "Aviation Composites Company". The company has developed a new vacuum infusion VARTM process (Vacuuminfusion VARTM) for the "black" wing, which makes it possible to use composite materials for the MC-21 wing's central wing box, spar, skin and stringer. The overall use of composite materials for this machine has reached 40%. Let's analyze the characteristics of this technology in detail below.
 
1. MC-21 aircraft: Use VARTM technology to manufacture aircraftLarge composite bearing structure
 
1.1 Composite material autoclave process and non-autoclave process
 
As we all know, composite materials are being used in aircraft manufacturing, weapons, ships and civil industries on a large scale because of their high strength, high rigidity, heat resistance, insensitivity to temperature changes and long life. At present, the mainstream manufacturing method of composite material components in the aircraft industry is autoclave forming. This method requires pre-impregnated carbon cloth, and after semi-finished products are obtained, the parts are formed in the autoclave. At present, the all-composite wings of Boeing B787 and Airbus A350XWB in civil airliners are formed by this process. The big disadvantages of this method are high equipment cost, long forming time, short storage time for semi-finished products, etc. For example, the storage time of prepreg is 12 months under the standard low temperature of -19℃--17℃, and 20 days under 20±2℃. The storage time of the production workshop is only 10 days.
 
The other method is the non-autoclave forming process. The essence of this method is that the immersion and forming are carried out at the same time, which can shorten the production time, reduce energy consumption and production labor, so the cost of this process is lower. Vacuum infusion VARTM (Vacuum infusion VARTM) is one of them. In this technology, the resin is injected into the dry filament under vacuum pressure to form, so the pre-production preparation work for structural parts can be greatly reduced, and simple tooling can be used to reduce costs. However, the main disadvantage of this process is that the processing cannot be repeated.
 
Before 2006, the US aviation industry did not recommend using VARTM technology to manufacture some key parts on aircraft. However, VARTM technology has been continuously improved since then, and some new technologies have been developed on this basis. For example, the German company PremiumAerotec uses vacuum assisted molding (VAP) to manufacture the pressure bulkhead of the Boeing 787; Boeing Aerostructures uses pressure controllable resin infiltration molding technology (CAPRI) to produce vertical stabilizers, wings, tails, ailerons, flaps, etc.; Canada’s Bombardier uses liquid resin infusion technology (LRI) to manufacture the wings of the C-series aircraft; the composite center wing exhibited by British GKN in 2016 uses VARTM technology.
 
1.2 The manufacturing process of the MC-21 composite "black" wing
 
Generally speaking, the wing and tail of a narrow-body aircraft account for 45% of the total weight of the aircraft. Therefore, at the beginning of the design of the MC-21 aircraft, it was determined to use composite materials to reduce structural weight and production labor, and strive to have a place in the world aviation market. The Russian "Composite Material Company" avoided mainstream autoclave technology and chose non-autoclave VARTM technology, not only because of the low cost of VARTM technology, but also the overall integrated function of VARTM technology.
 
MC-21 adopts an international cooperative production mode in composite material technology. The company first cooperated with the Austrian Diamond Company and the FISCHER** Composites Company to manufacture a 40-meter-long wing box, conducted a connection test between the wing box and the central wing, and passed the strength test of the Russian Central Aerodynamics Institute. The test data confirmed that the design parameters can ensure flight safety and the large-scale integrated structure can reduce assembly labor, reduce the number of parts and rivets, etc.
 
In addition, in terms of materials and equipment, we have also chosen the cooperation of internationally technologically advanced companies. For example, Hexcel's carbon fiber, Coriolis composite company's robot automatic wire laying head, Spain's MTorres company's gantry type wire laying equipment, and France's Stevik company's TIAC perfusion center.
 


Dry carbon fiber robot laying head of Coriolis Composites


Dry carbon fiber gantry automatic laying machine of MTorres
The laying of composite material wings is very complicated, because the wings have to withstand different external loads, such as extrusion, stretching, torsion, etc., so the laying of composite materials must withstand the load strength from different directions. Multi-layer carbon fiber Lay them together from different angles. The Spanish company MTorres is very experienced in this area. Aerospace Composites and MTorres have done a large number of tests on materials, parts and prototypes. The selected Hexcel carbon fiber must not only be suitable for the layup of the central wing panel, but also need to meet the automatic laying requirements of the MTorres equipment. Although the automatic laying speed of Hexcel carbon materials can reach 50kg/h, it is necessary to ensure The foolproof laying speed has been reduced. In addition, dry fibers are not the same as prepregs. Dry fibers are easily misplaced during the automatic layup process. To solve this problem, MTorres uses a thin layer of thermoplastic filaments to fix the fibers like glue, but in order to prevent excessive For bonding, a heat removal system is also installed on the placement head.
 
MTorres company's dry fiber gantry robot automatic placement system with heat removal system
The infusion equipment uses TIAC equipment, which is an integrated equipment that integrates injection, heating and software control. It can monitor and control the temperature and quantity of the injected resin, the filling speed, the integrity of the vacuum bag and the preform, etc., vacuum The degree of control accuracy does not exceed 1 mbar. The forming time is about 5-30 hours according to the size and complexity of the part, the curing temperature is 180℃±2℃, and the high temperature is about 270℃.
 
TIAC Automated Perfusion Center of French Company
Wing box process:
 
1. Tooling preparation and auxiliary material laying
 
2. Carbon fiber dry filaments are layered and preformed in the tooling
 
3. Vacuum bag assembly
 
4. Intervene blank injection molding
 
5. Demoulding and part cleaning
 
6. Non-destructive testing
 
7. Machining and shape inspection
 
8. Painting and assembly
 
All work must be carried out in the clean room. After the spar is preformed, it is placed in the male mold, the skin is placed in the female mold, and the entire tooling is placed in a vacuum bag for vacuuming. A number of pipes are laid around the vacuum bag. glue.
 
Schematic diagram of vacuum infusion VARTM process
In the vacuum infusion process, the long stringers and the wall panels must be separately laid and pre-formed, but the glue injection and curing can be formed together in a dedicated tooling. The autoclave process needs to be divided into two parts: first curing the long stringer, and then curing together with the skin, so the autoclave process is 5% longer than the VARTM process, the energy consumption is more than 30%, and the VARTM injection The characteristics of glue can be used to manufacture integrated integral parts, instead of glue-rivet structure.
 
Spar in forming Central wing panel in forming
After the parts are cured, ultrasonic non-destructive testing is carried out, and the reliability of the parts is tested using the Technatom robot inspection system. Then there is a five-axis milling machine for machining.

2. Advantages of MC-21 composite "black" wings
 
2.1 The composite wing with large aspect ratio and thin airfoil saves more energy
 
The MC-21 wing aspect ratio reached 11.5. The large aspect ratio and thin airfoil composite wing make the MC-21 have higher aerodynamic efficiency compared with the same type of aircraft. At 0.78 Mach speed, the aerodynamic rate is 5.1% higher than Boeing 737 and 6% higher than Airbus A320; energy consumption is reduced by 8% compared with traditional aluminum alloy. The traditional mid-range trunkline aircraft with aluminum alloy wings burns 140,000 tons of fuel during its full life cycle (about 20,000 hours), while each MC-21 can save 11,000 tons of fuel during the same flight time.
 

MC-21 composite right wing


2.2 Lighter weight
 
Compared with aluminum alloy, the composite material has a lighter unit weight. In theory, using composite materials instead of metals can reduce weight by 15%. However, according to Russia, it seems that the composite wings of Boeing 787 and Airbus 350XWB have not reached the initial weight reduction target. Mainly because the Boeing 787 and Airbus 350XWB are designed with too much structural strength margin. Generally, the strength margin of aluminum alloy structure does not exceed 1.5, but the margin of composite structure reaches 5 or even 7. MC-21 has learned the lessons of its predecessors when designing, so the weight is lighter.
 
2.3 High economy
 
Vacuum infusion technology can achieve automation to a large extent, and can manufacture the overall structure, so the non-autoclave process instead of the autoclave not only simplifies the production process, but also saves time and reduces production costs.
 
3 summary
 
3.1 The autoclave process and the non-autoclave process are two technical routes that currently go hand in hand with composite manufacturing technology.
 
Although the MC-21 aircraft uses the non-autoclave process to produce large load-bearing parts of the aircraft, it is still too early to assert that the non-autoclave process will replace the autoclave process. MC-21 is still in flight test, and many design data have not been confirmed. In addition, the autoclave process is constantly improving, and automation and economy are its main development directions.
 
3.2 The international cooperative production model of high-tech products is increasingly being challenged
 
The MC-21 aircraft is a good example. In recent years, under the high-pressure sanctions imposed by the United States, the MC-21 aircraft had to accelerate the pace of localization. In May 2020, the Ministry of Industry and Trade of the Russian Federal Government and Irkut Corporation signed a contract worth 12 billion rubles to carry out research and experimentation on the substitution of MS-21 composite materials and engine imports. According to the contract, Irkut Company shall complete the localized manufacturing of composite components such as the wing spar, central wing box, rudder and tail wing by the end of 2020, and replace the imported prototype with the domestically-made PD-14 engine. The localized MC- will be completed in 2021. 21 Manufacture of test prototypes and carry out test certification.
 
The MC-21 aircraft made a good start, but how it will end will wait and see. MC-21 has also sounded the alarm for my country's high-tech manufacturing companies. In today's political environment, only those countries that firmly hold the core technology in their own hands can finally win.
