British company develops variable temperature curing epoxy resin prepreg

Category: Company headlines    release time: 2020-07-15    Author：admin
  share to:   
QR code sharing
    A few days ago, British prepreg supplier Composites Evolution has developed a variable temperature curing prepreg called Evoprep EPC200, further expanding its product range. Thanks to the newly developed toughened epoxy resin system, the product can be cured in a temperature range of 65-120°C through a vacuum bag, vacuum furnace, non-autoclave or molding process.


 

       The company emphasized that Evopreg EPC200 prepreg is mainly developed for manufacturers who use low-temperature non-autoclave compression molding processes to obtain high surface finish. This epoxy prepreg has a flexible curing curve and is suitable for rapid prototyping at high temperatures, shortening the production cycle while maintaining a high level of performance.

       Composites Evolution’s Evopreg EPC series resins can be widely used in reinforcements such as carbon fiber, glass fiber, aramid and natural fiber ampliTex.
