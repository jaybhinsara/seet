Experimental research on the forming process of hot autoclave by means of fiber grating

Through the optical fiber grating method, the development of the assimilation residual stress in the thickness direction and in the plane caused by the thermal mismatch between the metal assimilation mold and the composite material component during the molding process of the autoclave was studied, and the residual stress after curing was obtained. The distribution of stress along the thickness direction and in-plane of the component, and the generation mechanism of the residual stress distribution and the influence on the deformation of the component after assimilation are analyzed. The results show that the solidification residual strain caused by the thermal mismatch between the composite material and the mold presents a gradient distribution along the thickness direction of the component, which is closer to the mold end than far away from the mold end, and this strain will cause the assimilation of the warpage and deformation of the component. The fiber direction is dominant.


The thermal mismatch between the metal mold and the composite material component in the integrated molding process of the autoclave is one of the factors that cause the solidification residual stress and deformation of the composite material component. Under the action of the pressure in the autoclave, the composite material component is close to the surface of the mold. When the temperature changes, due to the mismatch of the thermal expansion coefficient, there will be an interaction between the mold and the composite material, resulting in shear stress, and along the component A stress gradient is formed in the thickness direction, which remains in the component as the resin cures.


Through experimental research, it is found that the composite material unidirectional flat plate has a large warpage after curing and forming, and it is believed that the warpage deformation is caused by the thermal mismatch between the mold and the component. At the same time, it is pointed out that the mold and the composite material are in the curing process. The relationship is neither completely bonded together nor purely friction, but a process of change. When the first interaction occurs, it can be regarded as bonded together, and as the interaction strengthens, the interface When the shear strain increases to a certain value, friction starts from the two ends of the component, and this interaction is very complicated, and the interaction under different material systems and process conditions is different.
