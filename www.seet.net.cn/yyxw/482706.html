DARPA develops customizable universal raw materials in the TFF program for low-cost composite materials

Category: Industry News    release time: 2019-11-23    Author: admin
  Share to:   
QR code sharing

 U.S. Department of Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) It has a long tradition and history in advancing the development of composite materials technology. His research projects include "Affordable Composites for Propulsion" (Affordable Composites for Propulsion) and "Non-autoclave molding materials" for propulsion systems. (Out of Autoclave Materials), etc., the research of these projects has made the 5320-1 series of prepregs have been widely used in the defense industry. In addition, DARPA has recently undertaken the TRUST project to study the bonding of composite primary structures.


 

       DARPA’s "raw "Trim and Forming" (TFF) is scheduled to be launched in 2015 and aims to achieve rapid, low-cost and flexible manufacturing of composite parts weighing less than 20 pounds (9.07 kg). In the use of large composite parts, for example, the use of automatic fiber placement and automatic belt lamination processes to manufacture large-size and reinforced skin parts, composite materials have shown better applicability than metal materials. However, in a typical tactical military aircraft structure, approximately 80% of the entire fuselage structure weighs less than 20 pounds (9.07 kg). And most of these parts are made of metal, because composite materials can only achieve a clear advantage over metal materials in the main defense components weighing more than 20 pounds (9.07 kg).

       To solve this problem In the TFF project, a customizable universal raw material (TuFF) for forming is being developed-a material that uses traditional polyacrylonitrile (PAN)-based carbon fiber to be cut into a specific length (. In the end, the plan hopes to use pitch-based carbon fiber) As well as highly aligned discontinuous fibers, these fibers can be customized in length, surface modification, matrix, and can be combined with other materials to achieve formability similar to sheet metal through rapid processes such as stamping. The TuFF sub-project is implemented by the leadership of the University of Delaware.


 

        forming process Comparison

       TuFF's parallel manufacturing It is a forming sub-project led by Boeing, named "RApid High Performance Molding" (RAPM). RAPM’s partners include not only companies in the aerospace sector, but also material suppliers Solvay Composites (Belgium) and thermoplastic composite structures...ATC Manufacturing Technologies, as well as more companies often associated with the automotive and industrial markets , Such as SGL Carbon (Germany) and pixelated temperature control...Surface Generation (UK), the company has provided an automated molding system that can be used to press molded parts for notebook computers As well as injection molded plastics and a series of composite parts.


 

        TuFF and RAPM Displayed 14 research results-including material performance testing of new materials and demonstration and verification of a series of challenging parts. The latter can make an intuitive comparison of materials and processes, including not only thermoplastic composite forming and thermosetting composite resin transfer molding (RTM), but also high-pressure RTM (HP-RTM) development similar to Audi and The low-pressure super RTM process often used in Voith Composites. A process for fixing 2D reinforcements using a spring-loaded frame to prevent wrinkles and other defects during 3D shape pre-forming/molding has also been developed. The production cycle benchmarks of all processes have been tested in less than 30 minutes. Some parts made of thermoplastic composite stamping can achieve production cycle times as low as 5 minutes.

        Surface generation company's automatic molding structural unit

        RAPM also evaluated The PtFS system with independent intellectual property rights of the surface generation company is suitable for various near-net-shape and net-shape aerospace thermosetting and thermoplastic composite materials processing applications. Modular processing of prepregs, organic sheets and dry fibers, the latter of which is used for resin infusion.

         interim study The results are described in detail in "Development of Scalable Dynamic Control Architecture for Flexible Composite Manufacturing Work Cells" by Steven Shewchuk of Boeing.

The surface generation company provides a fully integrated Customized PtFS splints, tools, and molding work cells can produce composite parts up to 0.9 m × 0.9 m. The work cell can be reconfigured to produce different parts within a few minutes, and has been tested in a variety of configurations, including vacuum, resin infusion, thermosetting, and thermoplastic composite pressure curing forming. The system has extensive and important parts processing data accumulation, but also has the ability of isothermal mixing and cycle processing.
